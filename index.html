The Cost of Rushing: Google Bard

−$100B market cap lost in one day

Feb 8, 2023. One factual error in a promotional video. One rushed launch.

On February 8, 2023, Google unveiled Bard in a promotional video. The chatbot incorrectly stated that the James Webb Space Telescope took the first pictures of a planet outside our solar system — that was done years earlier by ground-based telescopes. Experts spotted the error immediately. Alphabet stock dropped ~8% that day, wiping roughly $100 billion off the company's market cap in a single session. The irony: Google had the technology. They had been working on large language models longer than almost anyone. But competitive pressure from ChatGPT and Microsoft forced a rushed, under-tested public demo. The cost was enormous — both financially and reputationally. This is the exact pattern Inflectra chose to avoid.

The Market Speaks: PLTR

~2,000% gain from ChatGPT launch to peak

Palantir's rise reflects market sentiment, not a product pivot. The underlying platform barely changed — the narrative did.

But how much of that is hype?

Palantir IPO'd in September 2020 at $10. It ran to $45 in the post-COVID tech boom, then crashed back to $6 by the time ChatGPT launched on November 30, 2022. From that $6 floor, the stock rose nearly 2,000% to over $125 by early 2025 — purely on AI sentiment. Palantir's core product (government data analytics) barely changed. The market was pricing in an AI future that hadn't been built yet. This is the hype cycle in action. The question for enterprise software buyers is: are you buying the narrative, or the product?
